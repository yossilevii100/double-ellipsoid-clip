Abstract

Contrastive Language‚ÄìImage Pre‚ÄëTraining (CLIP) is instrumental across many domains, yet its embedding geometry is not fully understood. We examine the raw (pre‚Äënormalized) features and show that image and text reside on linearly separable ellipsoid shells, both shifted away from the origin. Key Insights

Double-Ellipsoid Geometry

Image and text features occupy distinct, shifted ellipsoid shells in the raw CLIP space; their separability explains the modality gap.

A sample's conformity‚Äîthe average cosine similarity to all others‚Äîcan be estimated by its cosine similarity to the modality mean.

Understanding this geometry motivates simple diagnostics and improved alignment methods in downstream tasks.
