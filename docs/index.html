Abstract

Contrastive Language‚ÄìImage Pre‚ÄëTraining (CLIP) is instrumental across many domains, yet its embedding geometry is not fully understood. We examine the raw (pre‚Äënormalized) features and show that image and text reside on linearly separable ellipsoid shells, both shifted away from the origin. We explain the benefits of this structure and introduce a notion of conformity, which closely matches cosine similarity to the modality mean. We further connect this geometry to the modality gap and the narrow‚Äëcone effect.

Key Insights

Double-Ellipsoid Geometry

Image and text features occupy distinct, shifted ellipsoid shells; their separability explains the modality gap and Narrow Cone Effect.

Conformity ‚âà Mean-Similarity

A sample's conformity‚Äîthe average cosine similarity to all others‚Äîcan be estimated by its cosine similarity to the modality mean.

Optimal Convergence

Practical Implications

Understanding this geometry motivates simple diagnostics and improved alignment methods for downstream tasks.
